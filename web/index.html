How it works

Four steps. No custodian in any of them.

From a signed order to funds on-chain, the operator is a pure matchmaker — it relays messages and never holds value. Every transition is verified, idempotent, and recoverable after a crash.

01 / intent

Sign, don't deposit

Your wallet posts a signed order intent to the relay. Nothing is locked or custodied — it's a price you'll honor, cryptographically.

signed · off-chain
02 / match

Cross on the book

The relay crosses your intent with another by price-time priority. No central counterparty, no order-book custody.

price-time priority
03 / handshake

Agree, byte for byte

Matched peers exchange keys and a secret hash over the relay and independently derive the identical swap — same lockup addresses on both sides.

deterministic terms
04 / settle

Atomic on-chain

One secret unlocks both legs across BTC and PRL. Hashlocks make theft impossible; the secret crosses chains as the swap consummates.

cross-chain atomic swap
Why trustless

Theft is impossible. Stalling is expensive.

The hard problems that have killed atomic-swap DEXs — custody, the free option, human arbitration — are answered with script, not trust.

// custody

The operator holds nothing

No funds, no keys, no quotes, no liquidity. It's an open relay that crosses signed intents — and one you can route around entirely. The venue earns a fee on volume, never by holding value.

// settlement

Atomic, not escrow

Both legs are scriptable, so a trade is a real cross-chain atomic swap. Neither side can steal; your principal is protected by a hashlock and a refund timelock on its own chain.

// the free option

Bonds, not arbiters

A forfeitable, secret-tied bond neutralizes the free-option problem that sinks naïve atomic swaps — walking away costs you. No governance, no human adjudication, no deposits held by a third party.

    You hold your keys, always

    The LP daemon never hands custody to the venue or the counterparty. It funds its own leg and reclaims with the swap secret — there is no pooled wallet to trust.

    The venue takes a fee; you take the spread

    A small operator fee rides on matched volume. Your margin is the spread you quote. Liquidity without the operator ever providing capital.
